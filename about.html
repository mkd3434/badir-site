The Founder

I run 8 businesses.
I built this because nobody else would.

My name is Mustafa Kivanc Demirsoy. I'm a penetration tester, a builder, and a Muslim. I run a cybersecurity company. I run an AI automation agency. I run a jewellery brand, a kebab restaurant, a marketplace, an escrow platform, and two SaaS products. Eight businesses across four industries.

I'm not telling you this to impress you. I'm telling you because every single one of those businesses started with the same moment: saying Bismillah and shipping something ugly.

WeHack started as a one-page website. LUA started as three rings on a Shopify store. ReRack started as a Lovable.dev prototype built in a weekend. None of them were perfect. All of them were shipped.

The gap I kept seeing

I'd go to Muslim events — conferences, meetups, MSA gatherings. Brilliant people everywhere. CS students, designers, marketers, engineers. All of them had ideas. All of them were "working on something."

Six months later? Same conversations. Same "I'm almost ready to launch." Same Notes app full of ideas, same portfolio empty of shipped products.

The gap isn't talent. It isn't funding. It isn't knowledge. The gap is that nobody is building daily. Nobody has a community that pushes them to ship — not once a year at a conference, but every single day.

Why Badr?

The name comes from the Battle of Badr — the first major battle in Islamic history. 313 Muslims faced over 1,000 Quraysh soldiers. They were outmanned, outfunded, outequipped.

Before the battle, some people left. They had their reasons. They had their doubts. But the 313 who stayed? They trusted Allah, tied their camels, and went to battle. They won.

That's the spirit I wanted for this community. Not "let's talk about building." Not "let's plan to maybe launch someday." But: say Bismillah, pick an idea, ship it this weekend.

بادِرْ
"Take the initiative. Be the first to act. Hasten to do good."

What I want Badir to be

A community where Muslim youth build things. Every day. Not because someone's watching — because the rhythm becomes who you are. You wake up, say Bismillah, and build.

I want the student with 47 ideas to ship one this weekend. I want the side hustler who got inspired at Muslim Tech Fest to actually finish something. I want the senior dev with 10 years of experience to mentor the next generation of Muslim builders.

I want 313 builders who ship. Not 10,000 followers who watch.

If that's you — if you're tired of talking and ready to build — come join us. The 313 didn't wait for perfect conditions. Neither should you.
