Sprint Format

Lightning Decision Jam
45 Minutes. Real Decisions.

Duration 45 Minutes
Format Remote (FigJam/Miro)
Outcome Decisions + Commitments

Why This Matters for Us

Most collaborations die in group chats. Ideas float, nobody decides, nothing ships. The Lightning Decision Jam fixes this.

In 45 minutes, we go from scattered problems to concrete commitments with deadlines. No lectures, no "let's circle back," no wasted time. Everyone participates equally — silent writing phases prevent loud voices from dominating, and voting keeps us honest about what actually matters.

What You Get Out of It

Real decisions, not discussions

Every session ends with specific, ownable action items. You leave knowing exactly what to do next — not "we should probably think about..."

Equal voice for everyone

Silent writing phases mean introverts contribute as much as extroverts. Voting is democratic. The best ideas win, not the loudest person.

Accountability that sticks

Public commitments: "By [day], I will [specific action]." When you say it out loud to the group, you follow through. Bismillah.

Unblock each other fast

Bring your biggest blocker. Get 5-10 solutions from the group in 8 minutes. The Effort/Impact matrix instantly shows you what to tackle first.

How It Works

0:00 — 5:00 • 5 min

Write Problems

Everyone writes blockers/problems on stickies. One per sticky. Silent. No discussion. Just dump everything that's stopping you from shipping.

Reveals: Who identifies real blockers vs vague complaints
5:00 — 8:00 • 3 min

Vote on Problems

3 dot votes each. Put them on the problems you think matter most. No campaigning. The group's collective intelligence picks the real issue.

Reveals: Who has taste — surface-level thinkers vs root-cause finders
8:00 — 10:00 • 2 min

Reframe as "How Might We..."

Top-voted problem gets reframed. "Revenue is flat" becomes "How might we increase MRR by 30% in 60 days?" Specificity is everything.

Reveals: Framing ability — builders think in possibilities, complainers stay stuck
10:00 — 18:00 • 8 min

Silent Solutions

Everyone writes solutions. Quantity over quality. Crazy ideas welcome. One per sticky. No talking — this prevents groupthink and lets introverts shine.

Reveals: Speed of ideation, systems thinking vs band-aids
18:00 — 21:00 • 3 min

Vote on Solutions

3 dot votes again. Best solutions bubble up democratically. No HiPPO (highest-paid person's opinion) problem here.

Reveals: Strategic thinking — do they vote for quick wins or long-term plays?
21:00 — 30:00 • 9 min

Effort/Impact Matrix

Plot top solutions on a 2x2 grid. X-axis = effort. Y-axis = impact. The magic quadrant: high impact, low effort. This is where real calibration shows.

Reveals: This IS the IQ test — can they estimate complexity accurately?
30:00 — 40:00 • 10 min

Action Steps

Top-right quadrant solutions get broken into concrete next steps. Who does what? By when? No vague "we should probably..." — specific, ownable actions.

Reveals: Who volunteers? Who breaks tasks down well? Who watches?
40:00 — 45:00 • 5 min

Commit

Each person states out loud: "By [day], I will [specific action]." Bismillah. This is the moment. Social accountability + spiritual grounding. No wiggle room.

Reveals: The ultimate filter — talkers disappear here, builders lean in
